The strategy: three lenses

I do not rely on a single indicator. My edge comes from combining three independent reads on the market and only acting when they line up.

Order flow

What is happening now

The live auction, read straight from the order book, footprint, and delta, plus volume and TPO profiles. It shows where real buying and selling is happening and which price levels the market accepts or rejects, such as the value area and point of control.

Gamma exposure

The regime

Options-dealer positioning (GEX) sets the market's behavior. Positive gamma means dealers dampen moves and price tends to mean-revert; negative gamma, below the gamma-flip level, means dealers amplify moves and trends extend. It tells me how the session is likely to act before I take a trade.

ICT

Structure & timing

Inner Circle Trader price-action structure: liquidity pools, order blocks, fair-value-gap imbalances, and the time-of-day windows when institutions are most active. It frames where and when a move is likely to originate.

In practice, the three stack into one decision:

1

Regime

Gamma exposure sets the context: a mean-reverting session, or a trending one?

2

Structure

ICT marks the key levels and liquidity where a move should begin.

3

Confirmation

Order flow confirms real participation before I enter.

4

Execute

Enter with defined risk, sized to the firm's rules.

Order flow

A professional order-flow platform built on raw market-by-order (MBO) data. I use it for volume profiles, TPO and market-profile charts, footprint, delta, and the live order book.

QuantData

Options data

Exchange-licensed options data: options order flow, dealer gamma exposure, and dark-pool prints. This is where I read the GEX picture that sets the regime.

TradingView

Price action

My charting and price-action analysis, where I map ICT structure, mark key levels, and plan each trade.
